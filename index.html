About

Born in Colombia, hailing from the small town of Le Vaud, Switzerland, and New York resident since 1997, I feel like I've always carried the fiery creativity of Colombia, the stubborn neutrality of Switzerland, and the relentless ambition of the United States.

With a natural penchant for the outdoors, I set out to the beautiful wild state of Maine to study evironmental biology and French Studies at Colby College. While I was at first interested in biological research, my summer internships at the University of Konstanz (2015 in BW,Germany) and at Bigelow Laboratory for Ocean Sciences (2016 in Maine, USA) introduced me to basic programming and data analytics, for which my apetite grew year by year.

Upon graduating from Colby, I retained my passion for the environment and the desire to make a difference in whatever way I could, and I briefly worked on a startup with some college friends to design and build self-sustainable tiny houses. While working on this project, I became exposed to web development as I helped design the website, and in the years after graduation, progressively refocused my career on computer science.

Having enjoyed working with data while working in the lab as an undergraduate, I briefly took up the position as an Education Coordinator at a New York charter school working with student and teacher data before accepting a position as a Publisher Support Associate at ITHAKA, where I currently work to pull data and build reports for our publishers using the JSTOR platform for academic content. This position has allowed me to solidify my knowledge of the SQL language, Tableau, Jaspersoft, and managing and organizing databases.

However, because of my interest in both front and back end development, I decided to participate in a 24-week bootcamp run by Trilogy and Columbia University to seriously jumpstart a career in development. My goal is to be able to use the newfound skills from this bootcamp and combine them with my work experience and education background in the biological sciences to help technology implement sustainable behavior and infrastructure.

As previously mentioned, there are few things I love more than being outdoors. I am a passionate skier and hiker, I love snow, mountains and cold weather (my favorite season is--by far-- the winter), but I guess I can enjoy a nice sunny day at the beach. That said, it might seem almost paradoxical to have moved to New York City, but my hopes and dreams are to make concrete jungles green from within.

Outside of my professional and outdoors life, I also love to play tennis and football (the real kind that is played with the feet) and enjoy playing the piano. To close, I am a huge fan of Roger Federer, Bayern Munich, and lots and lots of (Swiss) chocolate.
